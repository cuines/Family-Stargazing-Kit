# Family Stargazing Kit

## Purpose
The Family Stargazing Kit is a collection of resources designed to guide students and their families in exploring the night sky together. This project fosters cooperative learning and helps families understand astronomical concepts through hands‑on, interactive activities.

## Target Audience
The kit is primarily designed for 5th‑grade students and their families, but can be adapted for younger or older learners. It is also intended for teachers who wish to share these materials with their own classes and collaborate on improving the resources.

## Table of Contents
1. **Solar System Scale Model** – A printable guide showing how to use fruit or household objects to understand planetary distances.
2. **Moon Phase Observation Log** – A printable template for families to draw and label the moon each night for a month.
3. **How to Find North** – A simple instruction sheet for finding the North Star and cardinal directions without a compass.
4. **Glossary of Astronomical Terms** – Definitions of key terms like planet, star, constellation, galaxy, nebula, etc.
5. **Constellation Connect‑the‑Dots** – A printable worksheet for young learners to connect dots and discover constellations (added in Version 1.0).

## How to Use This Kit
- Download the files from the latest release.
- Print the activity sheets and logs.
- Follow the step‑by‑step guides with your family.
- Share your observations and questions with other families or your teacher.
